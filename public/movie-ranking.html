The Best Movies According to Abbaskheljee

My top 3 movies of all-time.

1.The Wolf of Wall Street

Inspired by the authentic and compelling true-life narrative of Jordan Belfort, the story chronicles his extraordinary ascent from a financially ambitious and highly driven individual to becoming an immensely prosperous and influential stockbroker, reveling in the opulent and extravagant lifestyle that accompanies immense wealth. However, this tale also delves deeply into his subsequent and dramatic downfall, which is marred by a series of illegal activities, unethical behavior, and widespread corruption. Ultimately, his actions bring him into a fierce and consequential confrontation with the relentless forces of the federal government, leading to a catastrophic unraveling of his once-glamorous and seemingly untouchable existence.

2.The Shawshank Redemption

A Maine banker, who was convicted in 1947 for the brutal murder of his wife and her lover, embarks on a profound and transformative journey during his lengthy incarceration. Despite the weight of his conviction, the banker steadfastly maintains his innocence, clinging to the belief in his own truth. Throughout this extended period of confinement, he strives to hold onto hope, finding solace and strength in simple acts of compassion and kindness, which help him endure the relentless challenges of his imprisonment.

Hacksaw Ridge

Desmond T. Doss, an American Army medic who served with unwavering courage during the harrowing Battle of Okinawa in World War II, stands as a remarkable figure in history. Despite the intense and brutal conditions of combat, Doss held steadfast to his deeply held conviction against taking human life, resolutely refusing to carry or fire a weapon. His extraordinary bravery, selflessness, and unwavering commitment to saving lives on the battlefield led him to perform miraculous acts of heroism, risking his own life repeatedly to rescue and care for wounded soldiers. In recognition of his unparalleled valor and the lives he saved, Doss became the first individual in American history to be awarded the prestigious Medal of Honor without ever firing a single shot. His legacy remains a powerful testament to the strength of personal conviction and the profound impact of nonviolence in the face of war.
